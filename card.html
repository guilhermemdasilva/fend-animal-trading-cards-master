Ocelot

The fur is short and smooth; the back is basically creamy, tawny, yellowish, reddish grey or grey, while the neck and underside are white.

  • Scientific Name: Leopardus pardalis
  • Average Length: 75 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 20 years
  • Habitat: tropical forest, thorn forest, mangrove swamps and savanna

The ocelot is a medium-sized spotted cat. Between 55 and 100 centimetres (22 and 39 in) in head-and-body length and weighs 8–16 kilograms (18–35 lb). Carnivores, ocelots prey on small mammals. They are distributed extensively over South America.
